Free and Lower-Cost Stuff for Oregon 501(c)(3) Organizations

Posted by Jim on February 27, 2017


The State of Oregon offers a lot to local nonprofits operating as tax-exempt 501(c)(3) organizations—at least those that have an IRS exemption letter in hand. Here are some resources you may not know about:

Free Geek is a nonprofit that recycles computer and electronic equipment. Its Hardware Grants Program "connects qualifying non-profits with needed and available refurbished computer equipment." The program requires an application and has some limitations, but the program boasts having gifted "10,500 PCs and many other technology items" since 2000.

Some 501(c)(3) organizations helping the homeless, impoverished, elderly, or veterans, as well as museums and public health nonprofits might be able to obtain free or low-cost surplus government equipment, including "office furniture, generators, vehicles/heavy equipment, boats, power equipment, firefighting equipment, camping supplies, kitchen equipment and emergency supplies just to name a few." Generally you will need to apply and file a periodic report stating how the equipment is bieng used. The state website says that the program may help with transportation arrangements from Salem. (Washington State has a similar program.)

TechSoup is a nonprofit that provides low-cost hardware and popular software subscriptions to eligible 501(c)(3) organizations. Its large library of software includes Adobe and Microsoft products, as well as selections of software particularly appealing to nonprofits, such as fund-raising software.

The Nonprofit Association of Oregon is a large subscription-based nonprofit in Oregon. Paying subscribers have access to a range of free and low-cost services, especially information around regulatory compliance and growth. Chances are that the staff and volunteers at NAO can guide you to the resources you need. Follow NAO on Twitter (@NonprofitOregon) to keep on top of the changing legal landscape of Oregon nonprofits.

If you are interested in nonprofit news for Portland nonprofits, check out The Portland Society Page, a news service that covers the nonprofit scene. The site states that subscriptions are free for nonprofits.

Finally, bookmark the Oregon Secretary of State's resource page for nonprofits and check it periodically.
