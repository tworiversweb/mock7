The New 60-Day Rule for 501(c)(4)s

Posted by Jim on February 8, 2017


Background. Historically, 501(c)(4) “social welfare” organizations, which are exempt from income tax, sometimes never alerted the IRS that they exist. Although there have always been annual requirements to file an "informational" return (Form 990), some organizations never filed because, well, the IRS never knew that the organization existed.

Traditional requirements and consequences. Congress has long required the IRS to penalize organizations that do not file informational returns. Failing to file the appropriate paperwork risks (a) penalties and (b) revocation of tax-exempt status after missing three consecutive years. So 501(c)(4) organizations, like most tax-exempt organizations, need to file annual information returns.

The problem. The problem was that the IRS didn't require (c)(4)s to register with the IRS in the first place! It's hard to penalize or revoke tax-exempt status of an organization that you don't know about. Instead, an organization might simply introduce itself to the IRS with a Form 990 and “self-declare” that it was tax-exempt. No longer.

The new rule. The Protecting Americans from Tax Hikes Act of 2015 (PATH) set a new 60-day window for a new 501(c)(4) to notify the IRS that it exists and intends to operate as a (c)(4). (The new paperwork is Form 8976.") As of the date of this post, the filing fee for Form 8976 is $50.00 and it can only be filed electronically.

Applicable organizations. This 60-day window applies to most 501(c)(4) organizations formed after December 18, 2015 and some organizations already in existence on that date (namely those that the IRS didn’t already know about from previously submitted Forms 990 or 1024).

1024 still required. To be recognized as a tax-exempt (c)(4) still requires submitting Form 1024. The new form only alerts the IRS that a nonprofit exists that intends to operate as a (c)(4). (The 27-month look-back rule for submitting the application to be officially recognized as tax exempt, which applies to charitable nonprofits, might apply to 501(c)(4) organizations as well.

New penalties. The consequences of failing to send in this notification within the 60-day window are hefty penalties: $20.00 a day up to $5,000.00. The deadline for newer organizations was September 6, 2016.
